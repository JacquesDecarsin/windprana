Kitesurfing Disciplines

Discover the different kitesurfing disciplines and how we can help you progress in each one.

Kitesurfing Big Air

What is Big Air?

Big Air is the most spectacular discipline in kitesurfing. It involves performing high-amplitude jumps, sometimes over 20 meters high, and impressive aerial tricks. Riders typically use twin-tip boards and powerful kites to maximize their height and airtime.

Key improvement points in coaching

  • Jump technique: Learning perfect timing for takeoffs and landings
  • Power management: Mastering the kite in strong wind conditions
  • Trick progression: From simple jumps to advanced rotations (Handle Pass, Board Off, KGB)
  • Safety: Techniques for controlled and safe landings
  • Reading conditions: Identifying perfect moments to maximize height

Strapless Kitesurfing

What is Strapless Kitesurfing?

Strapless kitesurfing is the fusion between surfing and kitesurfing. Riders use a surfboard (without footstraps) powered by a kite. This discipline combines the gliding sensations of surfing with the freedom and power of kitesurfing, offering a unique experience on the water.

Key improvement points in coaching

  • Balance and positioning: Mastering the board without foot attachments
  • Jibe and transition techniques: Fluid and controlled turns
  • Wave riding: Using the kite to increase power in waves
  • Specific tricks: 360s, Shove-its, and other strapless-specific moves
  • Board recovery: Techniques for retrieving your board after a fall

Kite Foiling

What is Kite Foiling?

Kite foiling is the most recent and revolutionary discipline in kitesurfing. It involves using a hydrofoil under the board that allows riders to "fly" above the water. This hydrodynamic system offers an incomparable, silent, and resistance-free gliding sensation, even in light wind conditions.

Key improvement points in coaching

  • Take-off and control: Techniques for lifting the board out of the water and maintaining flight
  • Balance and weight distribution: Subtle adjustments to maintain stability
  • Foil management: Height control and fall prevention
  • Light wind riding: Techniques for riding in light wind conditions
  • Jibes and transitions: Foil-specific turns to maintain flight
  • Progression to tricks: Introduction to foil-specific tricks
